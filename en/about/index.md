*Out of Context* is written by Dr. Thorsten Fuchs and Anna-Sophie Fuchs.

## Dr. Thorsten Fuchs

Thorsten championed the "New World of Work" for two decades — then lived through its unintended consequences. After earning a PhD in pure mathematics he assumed he would never use again, he joined McKinsey & Company, where he learned how difficult it is to keep precision alive inside corporate systems. As the Office Business Group Lead at Microsoft Germany, he promoted offices designed for collaboration that would empty overnight in 2020. His 2014 book *Out of Office* saw part of the future of remote work clearly and missed the structural question this book now asks: what happens when flexibility arrives without enough context?

The irony is not lost on him: the mathematical framework he had left behind helped explain why similar organizational failures kept recurring under different names. Now, with the hindsight of failure and the tools of mathematics, systems thinking, and field observation, he maps the gap between what we promised and what we built. He lives near Munich, where the Klaus Brandl thread reminds him daily that some structures endure because they respect human nature.

## Anna-Sophie Fuchs

Anna-Sophie entered the workforce just as the promises of "new work" were colliding with their operating reality. With a master's in underwater archaeology, she is trained to excavate buried structures and reconstruct what sank them — skills surprisingly applicable to modern organizations. Where others see isolated initiatives, she sees patterns. Where others see progress, she sees sediment layers of abandoned assumptions.

Her research on context collapse began as academic curiosity but became visceral reality as she watched her generation inherit workplaces optimized for everything except actual work. She brings an archaeologist's patience to organizational analysis: careful excavation, meticulous documentation, and the humble recognition that today's innovations are tomorrow's artifacts. Her midnight kitchen-table revelation that ten organizational collapses were actually one pattern seen from different angles became the foundation of this book.

## Working Together

Together, Thorsten and Anna-Sophie work as independent researchers, writers, and advisors to organizations willing to examine work honestly. Since this book first appeared, that research has grown a spine of its own: the seven-volume *Panta Rhei* series on categorical foundations (December 2025; [panta-rhei-books.org](https://panta-rhei-books.org)), for which the first-edition *Fun with Functors* essay was the organizational overture and *The Functor Lens* is now the bounded second-edition companion.

Their professional collaboration remains possible through a strict household policy: work arguments end at the brewery door. They live with their family near Munich, where they practice what they preach — building boundaries that enable rather than constrain, maintaining context across generational perspectives, and remembering that craft beats theater every Tuesday morning.
